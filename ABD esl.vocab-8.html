Yawn

-Yawning is involuntarily opening the mouth and taking a long, deep breath of air. This is most often done when you are tired or sleepy, they are often contagious.

Breeze

1. A light gentle wind.

2. Easy - No problem.

Tenderness

-Gentleness & Kindness. Sensitivity to pain; soreness, Soft (being soft).

Devised

-Plan, develop or invent (a complex procedure, system, or mechanism) by careful thought.

Tattletale

-A person, especially a child, who reveals secrets or informs on others; a telltale.

Solemnly

-Serios; with a deep sincerity.

-In a formal and dignified manner.

Serene

-Calm, peaceful, and untroubled; tranquil, magical

Exasperated

-Annoyed, intensely irritated and frustrated, especially because you can do nothing to solve a problem.

Shrugged

-Raise (one's shoulders) slightly and momentarily to express doubt, ignorance or indiference.

Reluctantly

-In an unwilling and hesitant way, you don't wan't to do, but you do.

Stroking

1.Move hand/object with gentle pressure over (a surface/body), typically repeatedly; caress, touch, rub, brush.

2. Stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. Brain- cells begin to die in minutes.

-Physics: It demonstrates how to magnetize a metal - by repeatedly stroking it.

Invariably

-Always, on every occasion or case.

Distinguishing

-Something (quality) that makes someone or something different, unique or special

Discarded

-To throw something away or get rid of (someone or something) because you no longer want or need it

Snipped

1.cut (something) with scissors or shears, typically with small quick strokes. 2. If someone snipes at you -> they criticize you.

Cringe

-Have an inward feeling of acute embarrassment or awkwardness.

Glancing

1. Short, quick look. 2. Hit someone or something at an angle and bounce off obliquely.

Retroactive

-Usually in Legislation, rules and Laws: taking effect from a date in the past, before will also count.

Acquisition

-The act of acquiring or gaining possession, to get something.

Beaming

-Shining, grinning, really happy, smiling broadly.

Lash

1. Strike or beat with a whip or stick, 2. Eyelashes.

Lash out

-Attacking/exploading to the person (by words) anger- The Last Straw.

Escalating

-To become or make something become greater or more serious.

Lapse

-An accidental or temporary falling or slipping from a previous standard.

Startled

-Feeling or showing sudden shock or alarm.

Hush

-Make (someone) be quiet or stop talking.

Curb

1. A rim, especially of joined stones or concrete, along a street or roadway, forming an edge for a sidewalk.

2. A check or restraint on something, a limit on something that is not wanted.

Impulse

1. a sudden strong and unreflective urge or desire to act.

Meticulously

-In a way that shows great attention to detail; very thoroughly.
